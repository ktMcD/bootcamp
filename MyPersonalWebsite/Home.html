Motorcycling

There's a reason for rain gear

Geek on a bike says, "There's a special thrill in finding a new twisty road to straighen out"

The most important contributor to my enjoyment of any hobby is gear. It's *all* all about the gear.

I've lived in the Pacific Northwest all of my life. One thing I know for sure: I will get wet. When I'm on my bike, especially, I know that dark cloud ahead has my name on it. Gear, baby. Lots of it. Bags. Rain gear. Gloves. Heated clothing. Best helmet available in my budget. It all adds up to ensure that I'm not riding distracted by physical discomfort and have all of my attention available for staying out of the way and out of the hospital.

Yarn and yarn accessories

Sticks & String

The closest I will ever get to magic is being able to make a thing out of another thing

I'm katiekatt3 on Ravelry.com.

Let's talk a little more about gear. We tend to scrimp when we buy our first setup for any new hobby whether it's a cheap guitar, a scrappy used rat bike to learn on or plastic knitting needles. This approach, imho, isn't conducive to really enjoying your new hobby. That scrappy rat bike isn't going to get you very far and it might not even be safe. It's for sure that it's not going to imbue you with a tickly sparkly feeling when you ride it. Same with knitting supplies. Buy good yarn. Buy good needles right out of the gate. You'll have enough to contend with when you're first learning. You don't need to fight with your gear, too.
